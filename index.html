Dr. Vijay Victor studies behaviour, health, and public policy.

Assistant Professor in the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at IIT Roorkee. My work combines behavioural economics, health economics, applied econometrics, and field-based policy evaluation to study how people make decisions under risk, uncertainty, scarcity, and institutional constraints.

Research agenda

Applied economics for real policy problems.

My research is organised around empirical questions where behavioural mechanisms, household constraints, and public policy design intersect. I use causal inference, discrete choice methods, field experiments, and applied microeconometrics.

Health economics and insurance design

Publicly financed insurance, out-of-pocket expenditure, pharmaceutical coverage, missing-middle households, and health-seeking behaviour.

Behavioural economics and nudges
