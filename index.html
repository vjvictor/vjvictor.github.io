Economist working on behaviour, health, and public policy.

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee. My research examines how households and individuals make decisions under constraints, with particular attention to health insurance, out-of-pocket expenditure, maternal nutrition, climate-sensitive disease risks, dishonesty, nudges, and policy design. Methodologically, I work with causal inference, field experiments, discrete choice methods, and applied microeconometrics.

Research

My research agenda sits at the intersection of behavioural economics, health economics, and empirical policy evaluation. I am especially interested in how behavioural frictions and institutional design affect welfare-relevant decisions in health, insurance, and household finance.

Health insurance and household welfare Publicly financed health insurance, outpatient pharmaceutical coverage, out-of-pocket expenditure, and missing-middle households.
Behavioural interventions Nudges, choice architecture, maternal diet, dishonesty, time preferences, and decision-making under scarcity.
Causal inference and policy evaluation Difference-in-differences, field experiments, discrete choice experiments, and applied econometric research designs.
Climate and health risks Climate-induced dengue risk, disease proliferation zones, risk perception, and targeted public-health interventions.
